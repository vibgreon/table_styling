For transportation, transit options such as the BTS Skytrain and MRT Subway are convenient and affordable ways to get around the city. Taxis and tuk-tuks are also readily available, but be sure to negotiate prices beforehand. Walking is a good option for short distances, and biking is a fun way to explore some of the city's more scenic areas. Additional local insights and tips: - Dress appropriately when visiting temples and other religious sites. This means covering your shoulders and knees, and removing your shoes before entering. - Be aware of pickpockets, especially in crowded areas like markets and tourist attractions. - Be mindful of the heat and drink plenty of water to stay hydrated. It is also a good idea to wear sunscreen and a hat. - Thai street food may look and smell tempting, but be cautious of hygiene and cleanliness. Look for busy stalls with a high turnover of food or ask locals for recommendations.
